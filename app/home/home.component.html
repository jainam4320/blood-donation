According to the World Health Organization (WHO), India suffers from an annual deficit of two million blood units, as only 1% of Indian population donates blood each year.

    • 1. Red cells, platelets, plasma, and cryoprecipitate are the four types of transfusable products that can be derived from blood.

      2. Separating the whole blood into its various components can allow a single unit of blood to benefit multiple patients, and this process of donating specific components is called Apheresis.

      3. AB+ is the universal recipient of blood, which means AB positive persons can receive blood from any blood group. while O negative is the universal donor, which means O negative persons can donate blood to persons with any other blood group.

      4. Donated platelets must be used within five days and red blood cells must be used within forty two days from the date of collection.

      5. Blood products like plasma and cryoprecipitate are stored in a frozen state and thereby these products can be used for up to one year after collection.

      6. All donated blood products have to be screened for HIV, hepatitis B, hepatitis c and syphilis before transfusion.

      7. Blood transfusions are often used for supportive care in various surgeries. In lower and middle income countries especially they can also be used in the management of pregnancy-related complications, childhood malaria complicated by severe anemia and trauma-related injuries.

      8. Unnecessary transfusions can expose patients to HIV, hepatitis and adverse transfusion reactions.
